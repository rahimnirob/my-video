# Billboard Promo — Phase 4 Plan

## Phase 4: Archive Permanence — Show what lasts

### Goal
Introduce the most important differentiator of Billboard: it does not disappear after a short burst of attention. It becomes part of a lasting archive.

### Core idea
The viewer should understand that Billboard is a place where launches continue to live, not a one-time moment.

### Visual concept
Transition from the live broadcast surface into a premium archive grid. This should feel orderly, polished, and enduring.

### What should appear on screen
Use a simple, structured archive layout:
- a grid of past product cards
- one highlighted card with an orange glow or border
- a short archive label above or near the grid
- a single line that makes the point clear

### Motion language
The transition should feel calm and important, not flashy.
Use:
- a clean transition from the player to the archive surface
- subtle card emergence
- gentle highlight on one card
- a quiet but confident reveal

This should feel like a system that keeps working after the moment is over.

### Timing
Suggested timing:
- 0:12.5–0:17.5

This should be a strong proof beat for the long-term value of Billboard.

### Design notes
- Keep the archive grid readable
- Do not overload it with too many cards
- Highlight one card only
- The visual should feel curated, not random

### Typography and UI cues
- Use a minimal archive label above the grid
- Use a short line like “Your slot lives here” or a similar variant
- Keep the copy direct and simple

### Motion notes for implementation
Use a layered progression:
1. Transition from live player to archive state
2. Archive cards appear in a structured way
3. One card is highlighted
4. One closing line appears
5. Hold briefly for the value to land

### What this phase must achieve
By the end of this phase, the viewer should feel:
- Billboard is persistent
- it creates long-term visibility
- it is more valuable than short-lived attention

### Implementation guidance for Cursor
Treat this as a proof-of-permanence scene.
The archive grid should feel elegant and deliberate.
Do not overanimate it.

### Cursor prompt
Create a premium archive scene for the Billboard promo. Transition from the live broadcast surface into a structured archive grid of past launches. Highlight one card with a subtle orange glow, add a short archive label, and make the scene feel calm, curated, and permanent. The goal is to communicate that Billboard keeps working beyond the initial moment.

### Success criteria
- The archive feels premium and organized
- The permanence message is immediately clear
- The scene feels like a meaningful proof point
- The next phase can move naturally into the offer/value explanation
