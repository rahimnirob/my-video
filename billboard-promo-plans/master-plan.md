# Billboard Promo — Phase-by-Phase Build Plan

## Goal
Build a premium 35s Billboard promo that feels cinematic, modern, and product-real rather than abstract. The video should feel inspired by the inspo in motion language and atmosphere, but still be clearly its own story: from dead launch → alive Billboard → permanent archive → value → close.

## Core Direction
- Keep the video direct and mute-first.
- Every beat should answer one question visually.
- Prefer fewer, stronger beats over many decorative ones.
- Use the black void + orange glow as the emotional frame.
- Make the Billboard surface feel real and alive early.

## Shared Visual Language
- Background: pitch black void with controlled orange bloom and subtle grain/atmosphere.
- Typography: bold Sora for headlines, mono for telemetry/UI, Manrope or neutral body text for supporting copy.
- Motion: large-scale reveal, restrained transitions, confident holds, subtle blur, selective glow, no over-animation.
- Product truth: use the live Billboard surface, the player frame, the telemetry strip, the product info card, and the archive grid as the hero visuals.

## Phase 0 — Foundation & Visual System
Time: 0–60f

Purpose:
Establish the world and reusable motion system before scene content is fully layered.

Build goals:
- Lock the dark black background system with one controlled bloom source.
- Make the orange glow feel premium and intentional, not noisy.
- Set up a reusable text hierarchy and a few shared motion primitives.
- Prepare a base for hero reveals, text pushes, and UI fades.

Visual beats:
- Start with a near-black void.
- Introduce a single soft orange bloom that feels like a live signal, not a flat gradient.
- Add a subtle grain/atmosphere layer.
- Keep the first 2 seconds visually quiet and confident.

Motion notes:
- Use scale + blur + opacity, not multiple competing effects.
- Make the first reveal feel like a camera opening rather than a text animation.

Cursor prompt:
Create a clean foundational visual system for the Billboard promo. Use a near-black void with a controlled orange bloom, subtle cinematic atmosphere, and a premium motion setup. Build reusable animation primitives for hero text reveal, soft fade-in, blur-on-entry, and clean card emergence. The style should feel premium and modern, not cluttered or overly flashy.

## Phase 1 — Hook: The launch is dead
Time: 60–150f

Purpose:
Open with a feeling of loss and drop-off without making the video feel negative or boring.

Visual beats:
- A single word: LAUNCHED. or LAUNCH.
- The word is huge, centered, and slightly dimmed.
- Under it, a timestamp or tiny label appears like a dead memory: 2 days ago.
- The word doesn’t simply fade; it dims, drops, and loses energy.

Motion notes:
- The hero word should feel like it is sinking rather than disappearing.
- A tiny downward drift plus opacity loss yields a strong emotional cue.
- Keep the background minimal and the word alone in the frame.
- This is not a “marketing sentence”; it is a visual problem statement.

Cursor prompt:
Create the opening hook for the Billboard promo. Show one massive word in the center of a pitch-black frame with a soft orange glow. The word should feel like it is losing energy: slight downward drift, dimming, and reduced glow, as if the launch has already gone cold. Add a tiny timestamp or dead-state detail underneath to reinforce the idea without adding clutter. Keep it elegant and cinematic.

## Phase 2 — Reveal the alive surface
Time: 150–300f

Purpose:
Immediately pivot from the problem to the solution by showing the real Billboard surface.

Visual beats:
- Cut to black, then reveal the Billboard UI surface.
- Show the on-air header, telemetry strip, and player frame.
- The motion should feel controlled and premium, like a live broadcast suddenly waking up.
- This is the first real “proof” moment.

Motion notes:
- The transition should feel like an aperture opening.
- The player should arrive with glow, not a flat fade.
- Let the telemetry details read clearly and quickly.
- This should feel like the product exists, is active, and has state.

Cursor prompt:
Create the first product reveal scene. Transition from the dark hook into a premium live Billboard interface. Show the on-air header, telemetry strip, glowing player frame, and a subtle live-state atmosphere. Use a confident reveal, strong orange accent, and a polished broadcast feel. The purpose is to make the viewer immediately understand that Billboard is real, active, and alive.

## Phase 3 — Make the product feel real
Time: 300–420f

Purpose:
Show what the viewer is actually watching: product content in the player, a product card, a live status, and motion in the UI.

Visual beats:
- Zoom slightly into the player.
- Display the product info card: “NOW BROADCASTING” plus product name and one-liner.
- Show a progress bar filling.
- Add a ticker or small live line underneath the player.
- This proves that the product is not just a placeholder; it is a real broadcasting surface.

Motion notes:
- The player should become the visual anchor.
- Keep motion subtle and purposeful: slow push-in, gentle pulse, small UI transitions.
- The info card should appear as if it is part of the environment, not as an overlay pasted on top.
- Avoid too many secondary elements around it.

Cursor prompt:
Create a scene that makes the Billboard player feel tangible and real. Zoom into the live player, reveal a product info card with a live broadcast label, show a progress bar filling, and add a small ticker or live-state line beneath it. Keep the motion elegant and restrained, and make the interface feel like a working product surface rather than a mockup.

## Phase 4 — Prove permanence with the archive grid
Time: 420–540f

Purpose:
Move from “alive now” to “still matters later.” This is the most important differentiator of Billboard.

Visual beats:
- Transition from the player into a clean archive grid.
- The grid should feel curated and premium.
- One card is highlighted with glow or border emphasis.
- Add a simple label: Season archive, archived products, or a similar clear phrase.
- Finish with one line that explains the value: your slot lives here.

Motion notes:
- The archive reveal should feel like a calm but important expansion.
- The grid should not be too dense; it should feel organized and focused.
- A subtle glow around one card is enough to show that this is a persistent, valuable place.
- This scene should feel more like a proof of permanence than a decoration.

Cursor prompt:
Create the archive proof scene. Transition from the live broadcast surface into a premium archive grid. Display a structured grid of past broadcasts, highlight one card with a subtle orange glow, and add a clear label that makes the permanence obvious. The motion should feel calm, organized, and confident, showing that Billboard is not temporary.

## Phase 5 — Explain what the user gets
Time: 540–660f

Purpose:
Convert the proof into clarity. This is where the viewer understands what they receive.

Visual beats:
- A centered feature card or stacked list appears.
- Show a few very high-value points:
  - 30-day broadcast rotation
  - permanent archive
  - priority or amplification
  - analytics or live visibility
  - template production
- Keep the list limited and strong.
- One line should feel like the headline: what you get.

Motion notes:
- Build the list line by line with clean timing.
- Use a restrained card entrance and subtle stagger.
- Avoid too much text on screen at once.
- The list should feel like a concise offer, not a dense feature dump.

Cursor prompt:
Create a value scene that turns the product proof into a clear offer. Show a clean feature card or stacked list with a few premium benefits, one by one, in a simple and elegant rhythm. Keep the language concise and direct. The motion should feel polished and confidence-building, not overloaded.

## Phase 6 — Remove the last objection: no video needed
Time: 660–750f

Purpose:
Address the biggest founder objection in one simple, elegant beat.

Visual beats:
- A simple production panel appears.
- On one side, show a production brief or style options.
- On the other, show a video card with a play button or draft preview.
- A single line appears: No video? We build it.

Motion notes:
- This should feel calm and reassuring.
- The scene should not become too busy.
- The line should sit as the emotional payoff of the scene.
- This is a trust scene, not a feature scene.

Cursor prompt:
Create a simple production-support scene that removes the objection of “I don’t have a video.” Show a clean split-panel layout with a production brief on one side and a preview card on the other. Add one strong line, No video? We build it., and keep the motion calm, polished, and reassuring.

Visual beats:

- The final frame should feel calm, premium, and complete.

Motion notes:
- Keep the ending simple and spacious.
- The final line should feel earned, not shouted.
- Use a subtle glow and a restrained lockup.

Cursor prompt:
Create the closing scene for the Billboard promo. Return to a quiet black void and reveal the Avenir wordmark, the website URL, and one final line: Pay once. Stay visible forever. Keep the ending calm, polished, and confident, with just enough glow and spacing to feel premium.

## Implementation Notes for Cursor
- Reuse the existing Billboard promo scene structure as a container, but change the emotional logic.
- Prioritize the live Billboard surface and archive grid over abstract comparison scenes.
- Keep the pacing tighter and more decisive than the earlier version.
- Use fewer, more important text beats.
- Let the product surface carry the story.
- Avoid decorative transitions that do not help the viewer understand the product.

## What to simplify
- Remove abstract platform comparison loops unless they directly support the product reveal.
- Avoid too many kinetic words or purely decorative text bursts.
- Reduce scene count if a beat does not provide new proof.

## What to preserve from the inspo
- Controlled cinematic reveals
- Premium contrast and glow
- Strong hierarchy
- One hero element at a time
- A feeling of confidence and product reality

## What should feel different from the inspo
- The product story is different: this is about Billboard, not a general brand mood piece.
- The copy should be more direct and founder-friendly.
- The visuals should remain grounded in the actual product surface and its state transitions.
