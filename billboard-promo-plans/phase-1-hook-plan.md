# Billboard Promo — Phase 1 Plan

## Phase 1: Hook — The launch feels dead

### Goal
Create a sharp, cinematic opening that immediately feels premium and emotionally relevant. The viewer should understand, within a second or two, that this is about a launch that already lost momentum.

### Core idea
Do not open with a generic product slogan. Open with a feeling:
- the launch is already fading
- the product is not alive anymore
- this is the problem Billboard solves

The visual should feel elegant, dark, and slightly tragic — but still stylish.

### Visual concept
- Black void background
- One massive word centered in frame
- Strong orange glow, but restrained
- Slight downward drift and dimming
- A tiny dead-state detail under the word, such as a timestamp or old launch label

### Recommended on-screen text
Use one of these options:
- LAUNCHED.
- LAUNCH.
- LIVE? NO.

The strongest option is likely:
- LAUNCHED.

Why:
- it feels more like a state than a generic word
- it matches the emotional logic of “it was launched, but it is already dead”

### Motion language
The word should not simply fade in and disappear.
Instead, use a small emotional motion language:
- enter with a subtle push from below or from the center
- hold for a beat
- dim slightly
- drift downward
- lose glow
- maybe shrink a tiny bit

This should feel like a signal losing energy.

### Timing
Suggested timing:
- 0:00–0:02.5

That is enough to establish the problem without wasting time.

### Design notes
- Typography: bold Sora, oversized, very high contrast
- Color: near-white or warm off-white for the word
- Accent: soft orange glow around the edges, not too heavy
- Background: pure black or near-black, no clutter
- Atmosphere: subtle grain or tiny light bloom if needed

### Motion notes for implementation
Use a layered motion approach:
1. Opacity fade in
2. Slight scale-in from a slightly smaller state
3. Tiny downward translation
4. Soft blur or glow reduction
5. Gentle dimming of the word

The motion should feel cinematic, not mechanical.

### What this phase must achieve
By the end of this phase, the viewer should feel:
- this is not a generic promo
- this is about a launch that lost momentum
- the video has a premium tone
- the next beat will reveal the solution

### Implementation guidance for Cursor
Build this as a self-contained scene.
Do not add too many elements.
Do not animate too many layers.
The hero word is the whole scene.

### Cursor prompt
Create the opening hook for the Billboard promo. Use a pitch-black frame with one massive centered word, ideally “LAUNCHED.” or “LAUNCH.” The word should enter with a confident but slightly heavy motion, then lose energy through a small downward drift, dimming, and reduced glow. Add a tiny timestamp or dead-state label underneath to reinforce the idea that the launch is already fading. Keep the style premium, cinematic, and minimal.

### Success criteria
- The scene feels premium instantly
- The mood is clear within a second
- The hook is memorable and not generic
- The transition into the next phase feels natural
