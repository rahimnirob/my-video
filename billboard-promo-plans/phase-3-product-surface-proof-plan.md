# Billboard Promo — Phase 3 Plan

## Phase 3: Product Surface Proof — Make the product feel tangible

### Goal
Turn the live interface into a clearly understandable proof moment. The viewer should feel that the product is real, active, and watchable.

### Core idea
The product should stop feeling like a UI mockup and start feeling like a functioning broadcast surface with visible state.

### Visual concept
You are no longer just showing the interface. You are showing what the viewer is watching:
- a product playing in the player
- a product info block
- a progress bar filling
- a live ticker or status line

### What should appear on screen
Use the player as the main focus and add only a handful of supporting details:
- a product info card or overlay
- a short label such as “NOW BROADCASTING”
- product name and one-liner
- progress bar filling progress
- small live-state movement beneath or around the player

### Motion language
Keep motion restrained and elegant.
Use:
- slow push-in toward the player
- subtle pulse on the live indicator
- gentle progress bar fill
- small movement in the ticker or overlay
- slight zoom to emphasize that this is the important part

The motion should feel alive, not busy.

### Timing
Suggested timing:
- 0:07.5–0:12.5

This is the proof beat that should make the surface feel real.

### Design notes
- The player remains the hero object
- Keep the surroundings dark and controlled
- Use the product card as a realistic broadcast overlay
- The scene should feel premium and slightly cinematic

### Typography and UI cues
- Use Sora for the product title or main overlay title
- Use mono for live and telemetry details
- Keep the copy short and readable
- The overlay should look like it belongs to the product, not like a separate annotation

### Motion notes for implementation
Use a layered rhythm:
1. Slight push-in toward the player
2. Product info card appears
3. Live state and progress bar animate
4. Small ticker or lower-line movement begins
5. Hold briefly so the viewer can read the product state

### What this phase must achieve
By the end of this phase, the viewer should feel:
- this is not conceptual
- this is an actual active broadcast surface
- the product has presence and state

### Implementation guidance for Cursor
Make this a proof scene, not a decoration scene.
The focus should be on one central object: the player.
Everything else should reinforce its reality.

### Cursor prompt
Create a proof scene for the Billboard promo that makes the live player feel tangible and real. Zoom into the player, show a product info card with a live broadcast label, animate a progress bar filling, and add a subtle ticker or status line beneath it. Keep the motion elegant and restrained so the product feels active and believable rather than over-animated.

### Success criteria
- The player feels like a real living surface
- The product info card reads clearly
- The scene feels premium and grounded
- The next beat can transition naturally into the archive or permanence idea
