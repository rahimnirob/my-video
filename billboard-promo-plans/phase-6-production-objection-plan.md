# Billboard Promo — Phase 6 Plan

## Phase 6: Production Objection — Remove the last barrier

### Goal
Address the most common founder objection: “I don’t have a video.”

### Core idea
The product should feel like a complete service, not just a placement surface. If the viewer does not have a video, the system still works.

### Visual concept
A simple production-support scene appears. It should feel reassuring and trustworthy.

### What should appear on screen
Use a split or side-by-side visual:
- one side: a simple production brief or set of style options
- one side: a preview card or play button for a produced asset
- one strong line: “No video? We build it.”

### Motion language
The motion should feel calm and confident.
Use:
- a clean panel reveal
- a subtle preview card entry
- one strong sentence landing as the emotional payoff

The scene should feel supportive, not salesy.

### Timing
Suggested timing:
- 0:23.5–0:27.5

This should be a short but important trust beat.

### Design notes
- Keep the layout clean and readable
- Let the line do the emotional work
- Do not overload the scene with too much content

### Typography and UI cues
- Use a clean production label or brief structure
- Use a strong line in the center or above the panel
- Keep everything visually calm and premium

### Motion notes for implementation
Use a simple progression:
1. Production panel appears
2. Preview card or play button appears
3. The line lands
4. Hold briefly for the message to register

### What this phase must achieve
By the end of this phase, the viewer should feel:
- the product is complete and supported
- the friction is reduced
- the offer feels safer and easier to trust

### Implementation guidance for Cursor
Treat this as a trust scene.
The visuals should be calm and supportive.
Do not make it feel like an extra product pitch.

### Cursor prompt
Create a trust-building production scene for the Billboard promo. Show a clean production brief or style panel on one side and a preview card or video placeholder on the other. Add one strong line, No video? We build it., and keep the motion calm, polished, and reassuring.

### Success criteria
- The scene feels premium and reassuring
- The objection is addressed clearly
- The message is easy to understand instantly
- The ending can land cleanly after this
